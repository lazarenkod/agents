## OKR Scoring

### Grading Scale

**1.0 = 100%**: Fully achieved or exceeded
**0.7 = 70%**: Substantial progress, good outcome
**0.3 = 30%**: Some progress, fell short
**0.0 = 0%**: No progress

### Interpretation

**0.7-1.0**: üü¢ Success
- Ambitious goal largely achieved
- Stretch targets —Å reasonable completion

**0.4-0.6**: üü° Partial Success
- Made progress, –Ω–æ fell short
- Learn and adjust

**0.0-0.3**: üî¥ Miss
- Significant gap
- Retrospective needed
- Re-assess feasibility

```markdown

**Overall Objective Score**: (0.5 + 0.9 + 0.8) / 3 = 0.73 (Success!)
```

## Best Practices

### Writing Effective OKRs

‚úÖ **Aspirational not Incremental**: Stretch goals (70% success = good)
‚úÖ **Outcome not Output**: Measure impact, –Ω–µ activities
‚úÖ **Specific and Measurable**: Clear metrics, no ambiguity
‚úÖ **Time-Bound**: Quarterly cadence
‚úÖ **Limited in Number**: 3-5 objectives, 2-4 KRs each
‚úÖ **Aligned**: Support higher-level OKRs
‚úÖ **Transparent**: Visible to entire organization

### Common Anti-Patterns

‚ùå **Sandbagging**: Setting easy targets
‚úÖ 70% achievement = success shows ambition

‚ùå **Too Many OKRs**: >5 objectives = no focus
‚úÖ 3-5 max, prioritize ruthlessly

‚ùå **Activity-Based**: "Ship 10 features"
‚úÖ Outcome-based: "Increase engagement by 30%"

‚ùå **Set and Forget**: No progress tracking
‚úÖ Weekly check-ins, monthly reviews

‚ùå **100% Completion Expectation**: Treating as commitments
‚úÖ 60-70% = success for stretch goals

‚ùå **Individual Performance Evaluation**: Using for comp/promotion
‚úÖ Separate OKRs from performance reviews

## OKR vs Other Frameworks

| Framework | Focus | Cadence | Style |
|-----------|-------|---------|-------|
| **OKRs** | Aspirational outcomes | Quarterly | Stretch goals (70% success) |
| **KPIs** | Business health | Ongoing | Metrics to maintain |
| **MBOs** | Individual commitments | Annual | Performance-linked |
| **SMART Goals** | Tactical tasks | Varies | 100% achievable |

**When to use OKRs**: Strategic alignment, ambitious goals
**When to use KPIs**: Operational health, ongoing monitoring
**Combination**: OKRs –¥–ª—è strategy, KPIs –¥–ª—è BAU metrics
